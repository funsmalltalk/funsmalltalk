# Skill: Audience Research

## How to Find the Right Posts

### Reddit Search Strategy
Use these search queries (sort by "New" or "Hot", filter last 24-48h):

**r/socialskills**
- "small talk"
- "awkward silence"
- "don't know what to say"
- "networking event"
- "feel invisible"

**r/introvert**
- "social situation"
- "exhausting"
- "networking"
- "conversation"

**r/socialanxiety**
- "small talk"
- "party"
- "work event"
- "meeting people"

**r/careerguidance + r/cscareerquestions**
- "networking"
- "interview small talk"
- "awkward at work events"

### Post Quality Signals
Prioritize posts that have:
- Personal story or specific situation (not just general question)
- Some upvotes (5+) but not so big we'll be buried
- Comments showing others relate ("same here", "me too")
- No existing great answers (opportunity to add value)
- Recent (last 48h for Reddit)

### Quora Research
Search Quora for:
- "how to get better at small talk"
- "how to stop being awkward in conversations"
- "how do introverts network"
- "what to say when you run out of things to say"
- "how to approach someone at a networking event"

Prioritize: Questions with 1K+ views but weak or generic top answers.

### Signal Strength Rating
Rate each opportunity before drafting:
- **A** — Perfect fit: specific struggle, recent, no great answer yet, platform-appropriate
- **B** — Good fit: relevant topic, decent engagement
- **C** — Weak fit: too generic, too old, or heavily answered already

Only draft for A and B posts.
