# Skill: DM Writing

## Core Principle
DMs that work feel like they're from a real person who genuinely noticed something about you — not a template blast.

## Personalization Requirements
Every DM must reference ONE specific thing:
- The exact comment they left
- The specific post they engaged with
- The question they asked in a thread

Generic openers like "Hey, I noticed you're interested in social skills" are too vague. Kill them.

## Message Length Rules
- Message 1: 3-5 sentences max. Short = more likely to get read.
- Message 2: 2-4 sentences. Even shorter.
- Message 3: 4-6 sentences. Slightly longer because you're finally making the offer.

## Platform-Specific Rules

### Instagram DMs
- Casual and warm
- Can use one emoji if it fits naturally (not forced)
- Don't send a link in Message 1 — gets filtered as spam

### Reddit DMs
- Very conversational, no marketing language at all
- Reddit users are savvy — any whiff of a funnel and you lose them
- Never mention the course in Reddit Message 1
- If they ask "do you have a course?" — then yes, tell them directly

### TikTok DMs
- Very short
- Match the energy of the platform: casual, direct
- Reply to their comment first in public, then DM if they respond

## What Kills DMs
- "I noticed you might be struggling with..." (presumptuous)
- "I have a proven system that..." (marketer brain)
- Long intros about who you are
- Sending the link before they've expressed any interest
- Following up more than 2 times without a response

## Good DM Example (Instagram)
Trigger: Person commented "this is exactly me, I never know what to say at networking events"

Message 1:
"Your comment really resonated with me - that specific feeling of knowing what to say in your head but freezing up is something a lot of people don't talk about honestly. One thing that shifted things for me: I stopped trying to think of interesting things to say, and started asking questions I was actually curious about. Completely changed the energy."

Message 2 (if no reply, 3 days later):
"Just wanted to follow up - did the question thing help at all? I'm curious if it landed differently for you."
