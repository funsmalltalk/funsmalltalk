# Skill: Comment Drafting (Value-First)

## The Formula

**Good comment = Acknowledge + Relate + Value + Optional soft mention**

### Step 1: Acknowledge
Show you actually read the post. Reference something specific they said.
"This is such a common thing — especially that part about knowing what to say in your head but freezing up."

### Step 2: Relate (optional)
Brief personal connection — makes it human, not robotic.
"I spent years feeling exactly this way in networking events."

### Step 3: Value
Give them something useful right now. One of:
- A reframe ("The reason small talk feels fake is actually...")
- A quick framework ("One thing that helped me: before any event, I prep 3 open questions...")
- A counter-intuitive insight ("Awkward silences aren't actually awkward to the other person as much as they are to you — look up Spotlight Effect")
- Validation + direction ("What you're describing is really common after a few years of remote work. The skill just got rusty — it comes back faster than you think.")

### Step 4: Soft Mention (use sparingly)
Only include if:
- They directly asked for resources
- The comment is already long and the mention feels natural
- The platform allows it (Reddit is stricter — avoid in first comment)

Format: "I actually built a course on exactly this if you want to dig deeper — funsmalltalk.com. No pressure, the reply above should help either way."

## Length Guidelines
- Reddit: 100-300 words. Enough to be genuinely helpful. Not a wall of text.
- Quora: 300-600 words. Quora rewards depth.
- Instagram: 1-3 sentences. Punchy.
- TikTok: 1-2 sentences. Ultra punchy.

## Tone Check
Before finalizing, ask:
- Does this sound like a real person or a marketer?
- Would I be glad someone replied this to me?
- Am I adding value or just driving traffic?

If the answer to the last question is "just driving traffic" — rewrite.

## Red Flags (Don't Post These)
- Starting with "Have you tried [product/course]?"
- Mentioning the course in the first sentence
- Generic advice that could apply to any topic
- Copy-pasted replies (slight variation for each post is required)
